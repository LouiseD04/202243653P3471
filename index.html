Story 1: Looking to the Future, Yet Leaving It In The Past

For over a decade now, the SFA (Scottish Football Association) has flown the flag for producing talented youth footballers. They advocate that youth players aged 12-16 should be given core development and the facilities to do so to achieve their footballing potential. To this end performance schools opened their doors in 2012, however just over a decade later these schools are being mothballed. Following this ambitious programme to nurture youth talent, why are these schools now being phased out.

What are JD Performance Schools?

In 2012, the SFA decided to employ a new strategy to the develop talented youth players. In partnership with seven local authority schools across Scotland, they aspired to create facilities where youngsters could be educated whilst also gaining valuable minutes on the pitch. Thus performance schools were born which were designed to give talented young male and female footballers the opportunity to realise their ambition of playing professionally. Each performance school helps develop a pool of young footballers. Following a rigorous selection process, performance schools hand-pick players under the age of 12 to join their programme as they shift from primary to high school. Over a four-year period of secondary education, pupils gain up to 800 extra hours of coaching per year. Every school has a designated elite youth coach who is supported by national team and club coaches alike, who all contribute to the players’ progression and success. Geographically, pupils are most commonly selected from their local area and attend one of the performance schools within travelling distance of their home.
